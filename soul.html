NATURAL SOUL BROTHER: (IFP Finalist 2007) Drew DeNicola - director, editor, DP

NATURAL SOUL BROTHER IS THE STORY OF A FORGOTTEN CULTURAL ICON--THE BLACK RADIO DJ

By realizing the potential of radio- as a medium for preaching black empowerment as well as breaking color lines through the airwaves- they dazzled audiences with their charismatic rapping and mixing skills, re-inventing the medium and becoming the most powerful and recognizable celebrities in black America.

The film traces the evolution of black culture and music as it penetrates the popular American landscape and shows the unique role the DJ played in bringing this about. Through their stories a profound meditation on culture and commercialism emerges. Utilizing a patchwork of archival materials, personal stories, and the incendiary music of the time, Natural Soul Brother chronicles the rise and fall of the black radio DJ, the death of Soul Radio, and the resurrection in the late 70s South Bronx which gave birth to the top music genre of today, Hip-Hop.
